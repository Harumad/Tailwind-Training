The once-clear waters of the Pra, Offin , and Ankobra have turned murky and lifeless, threatening aquatic life, destroying farmlands, and endangering millions who depend on these waters for drinking and farming. The fight against galamsey is not just about land ‚Äî it's about the survival of our rivers, our people, and our nation's future.

Black Volta

The Black Volta River, once a powerful lifeline connecting Ghana's north to the south, now bears the marks of environmental distress. Communities who once depended on it for fishing and farming now face dwindling harvests and unsafe water. The Black Volta reminds us that every drop of water lost to greed is a future stolen from generations yet unborn.
